Our Projects

1. Urban Community Gardens

Our Urban Community Gardens initiative revitalizes vacant lots into lush, productive gardens that provide fresh produce and green space for urban residents. These gardens not only enhance local food security but also foster community spirit and environmental stewardship.

Goals:

  • Enhance access to fresh, nutritious food in urban areas.
  • Encourage community involvement and collaboration.
  • Promote sustainable gardening techniques.

Impact:

  • Developed 15 community gardens serving over 500 families.
  • Conducted workshops on urban agriculture for 2,000+ participants.
  • Transformed neglected spaces into safe, vibrant green areas.

Case Study: In Woodstock, we revitalized an abandoned lot into a thriving garden that provides fresh produce and serves as a community hub, fostering connections among neighbors.

2. Green Schoolyards Initiative

The Green Schoolyards Initiative partners with schools to transform their outdoor spaces into interactive, eco-friendly learning environments. These green spaces support environmental education and engage students in sustainable practices from an early age.

Goals:

  • Integrate environmental education into school curricula.
  • Create outdoor classrooms that enhance student learning.
  • Reduce schools’ environmental impact with green spaces.

Impact:

  • Worked with 10 schools, impacting over 3,000 students.
  • Planted 500 trees and shrubs, enhancing air quality and biodiversity.
  • Developed environmental education programs now integral to the curriculum.

Case Study: At Mitchells Plain Primary, we converted a barren playground into a vibrant garden and outdoor classroom, enriching science lessons and involving students in garden maintenance.

3. Waste Reduction and Recycling Workshops

Our Waste Reduction and Recycling Workshops teach urban residents how to minimize waste, recycle effectively, and compost at home. These workshops empower communities to adopt sustainable practices and reduce their environmental footprint.

Goals:

  • Raise awareness about waste reduction and recycling.
  • Promote composting and proper waste management.
  • Decrease landfill waste and environmental impact.

Impact:

  • Conducted over 50 workshops with 1,000+ participants.
  • Distributed 300 composting kits, supporting home composting efforts.
  • Reduced community waste by approximately 20 tons.

Case Study: In Khayelitsha, our workshops led to the establishment of a community-managed recycling center, fostering local recycling and providing economic benefits.

4. Urban Reforestation Project

The Urban Reforestation Project aims to enhance urban environments by planting trees to improve air quality and provide green spaces. This initiative also includes community education on the benefits of trees in cities.

Goals:

  • Expand tree canopy in urban areas.
  • Improve air quality and reduce heat island effects.
  • Engage the community in tree planting and care.

Impact:

  • Planted over 2,000 trees in urban neighborhoods.
  • Organized tree care workshops for 500 community members.
  • Collaborated with local authorities for long-term tree maintenance.

Case Study: In Salt River, our project planted 500 trees, significantly cooling local temperatures and enhancing the area's green infrastructure.

5. Sustainable Housing Workshops

Our Sustainable Housing Workshops guide residents in making their homes more energy-efficient and environmentally friendly. Topics include renewable energy, water conservation, and eco-friendly building practices.

Goals:

  • Encourage sustainable living practices in urban homes.
  • Reduce energy and water consumption.
  • Increase awareness of renewable energy and green building options.

Impact:

  • Trained 800 individuals on sustainable housing practices.
  • Helped 100 households install solar panels and rainwater systems.
  • Achieved a 15% reduction in energy use in participating homes.

Case Study: In Cape Town’s Southern Suburbs, our workshops led to significant home retrofits, lowering utility bills and reducing the community’s carbon footprint.
